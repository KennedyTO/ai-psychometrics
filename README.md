## Overview

This repository contains the analysis code and report for a psychometric evaluation of AI-generated psychological scales, conducted as part of my MA thesis research at York University. The study investigates whether items produced by large language models (LLMs) meet the same psychometric standards as human-authored measures — a question at the intersection of AI, measurement science, and applied psychology.

## Study Design

The study employs a **2 × 3 between-subjects factorial design**:

| Factor | Levels |
|--------|--------|
| **LLM** | GPT-5, Google Gemini Flash 2.5 |
| **Prompt Strategy** | Zero-Shot, Few-Shot-Basic, Few-Shot-Advanced |

This produces **six AI-generated Resolution scales** (6 items each, 7-point Likert), benchmarked against the **Zhang et al. (2025) criterion measure** of trust in AI (18 items across three subscales: Interaction, Truth, Resolution).

## Analytic Pipeline

The analysis proceeds through four stages, each building on the last:

| Stage | Method | Purpose |
|-------|--------|---------|
| **Inter-Item Correlations** | Pearson *r* matrices, Fisher *z*-pooling | Assess item-level coherence; flag weak or redundant pairs |
| **Reliability** | Cronbach's α (Feldt CI), McDonald's ω | Quantify internal consistency; identify underperforming items |
| **Exploratory Factor Analysis** | Principal axis factoring, parallel analysis, oblimin rotation | Determine dimensionality; verify unidimensional structure |
| **Confirmatory Factor Analysis** | ML estimation via `lavaan` | Test hypothesized factor structures; evaluate model fit |

For the criterion scale, all analyses incorporate **multiple imputation** (*m* = 20) with results pooled via **Rubin's rules**, including full FMI (fraction of missing information) reporting.

## Key Findings

- **Most AI-generated scales met conventional psychometric thresholds** for internal consistency, unidimensionality, and CFA model fit — comparable to the human-authored criterion.
- **Prompt strategy matters**: Few-Shot conditions (Prompts with adequate detail)  generally produced more cohesive item sets than Zero-Shot (Prompts with no detail).
- **Reverse-coded items generated by LLMs are problematic**: The Google Zero-Shot scale's reverse-coded item (Res_Item3_R) showed near-zero inter-item correlations, a low item-total correlation, a weak factor loading, and introduced an artifactual second factor — a consistent finding across all four analytic stages.
- **Practical implication**: AIG pipelines using LLMs should either avoid reverse-coded item generation or subject such items to rigorous pre-screening.

## About This Research

This work is part of a broader MA thesis investigating **automatic item generation (AIG) using large language models** — an emerging area at the intersection of AI and psychometrics. The research examines whether LLMs can serve as a scalable tool for generating measurement items that meet the standards required for psychological assessment.

The study was submitted to the **International Meeting of the Psychometric Society (IMPS 2025)**.
